Invited Lecturers

Prof. Nicholas Isaac Smith

Specially Appointed Associate Professor

Immunology Frontier Research Center, Osaka University

Presentation Title

"What has light done for us lately?"

Biography

Professor Nicholas Isaac Smith received his bachelor's degree from University of Sydney in 1997, and He obtained Ph. D. in applied physics from Osaka University in 2002. Following assistant professor in Osaka University from 2003 to 2009, he was appointed Associate Professor at Immunology Frontier Research Center (iFreC) , Osaka University in 2010.

His field of speciality is laser spectroscopy, and he is interested in interactions between laser beam and cells. He is active in study of mechanism of living cell with light or laser. Using light, he both observes and controls interactions between cells and physiological phenomenon, especially immune systems.

Prof. Hiroshi Yoshikawa

Professor

Nihon University

Presentation Title

"Computer-generated hologram for 3D display"

Biography

Hiroshi Yoshikawa received the B.S. degree, the M.S. degree and the Ph.D. from Nihon University, all in electrical engineering, in 1981, 1983 and 1985, respectively. He joined the faculty at Nihon University in 1985 where he currently holds the position of Professor of Electronics and Computer Science. From Dec. 1988 to Apr. 1990, he was a research affiliate of MIT Media Laboratory. His current research interests are in electro-holography, computer generated holograms, display holography and computer graphics.

Prof. Takashige Omatsu

Professor

Department of Nanoscience, Chiba University

Dean

Graduate School of Advenced Integration Science

Director

Molecular Chirality Research Center

Director At Large

Optical Society of America

Presentation Title

"Can twisting lights pioneer new generation materials science?"

Biography

Professor Omatsu received his B.S. and Ph.D. degrees in applied physics from the University of Tokyo, in 1983 and 1992, and he was appointed as a Professor in Chiba University, in 2007. He has been developing optical vortex light sources and pioneering nanostructures fabrication based on optical vortices illumination. He has published over 130 international journal papers, and he also presented over 100 invited presentations at international and domestic conferences.

He is currently playing a role as a Deputy Editor of Optics Express and a Director At Large of The Optical Society (OSA). Also, he is serving as a Visiting Professor of Xinjiang Normal University, China and Ajou University, Korea.

He was awarded prize for Science and Technology, The Commendation for Science and Technology by the Minister of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology in 2016. He was also elected as OSA Fellow and JSAP (Japan Society of Applied Physics) Fellow.
